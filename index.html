Bottom Line

Conclusion: In a nationwide Swedish cohort using a target trial emulation, initiating and sustaining ADHD pharmacotherapy was associated with lower rates of first occurrences of suicidal behaviours, substance misuse, transport accidents, and criminality; the estimate for first accidental injuries was not statistically significant. For recurrent events, reductions were observed across all five outcomes. Effects were generally larger among those with prior events and for stimulants versus non-stimulants.

PICO

  • PopulationSwedish residents aged 6-64 years with incident ADHD diagnosis (2007-2018); n=148,581 (41.3% female; median age 17.4)
  • InterventionStart ADHD drug treatment within 3 months of diagnosis and sustain treatment
  • ComparatorDo not start ADHD drug treatment during follow-up ("non-initiation")
  • OutcomesFirst and recurrent events over 2 years: suicidal behaviours, substance misuse, accidental injuries, transport accidents, criminality
  • DesignTarget trial emulation with cloning, censoring, and inverse probability weighting (per-protocol analogue)

Key Methods

  • Linked national Swedish registers (Patient, Prescribed Drug, Cause of Death, Crime, LISA).
  • Incident ADHD (ICD-10 F90); excluded prevalent users (â‰¥18 months washout).
  • Monthly adherence assessment; censor when deviating from assigned strategy.
  • Time-varying inverse probability of censoring weights with extensive baseline and monthly covariates; weights truncated at 99.5th percentile.
  • Weighted pooled logistic regression approximating incidence rate ratios; 500 bootstrap resamples for CIs.
  • Secondary: recurrent events; head-to-head stimulant vs non-stimulant; subgroup by age, sex, prior events; sensitivity (6-month grace, allow switches); negative control (type 1 diabetes).

Risk of Bias and Validity

  • Confounding: Extensive covariate adjustment with time-varying IPCW; negative control outcome showed null association (IRR â‰ˆ1.06).
  • Selection/Immortal time: Addressed via cloning and grace-period alignment; monthly censoring rules.
  • Misclassification: Dispensation â‰  ingestion; could bias towards null.
  • Measurement: Registers have high coverage/PPV; less severe outcomes may be missed.
  • Generalizability: Swedish setting with high methylphenidate use; external validity may vary.
  • Analysis: Per-protocol analogue informs effect of sustained treatment; effect sizes smaller than within-individual studies but more representative.

Critical Appraisal

  • Strengths: Nationwide data; rigorous target trial emulation; robust sensitivity analyses; clinically relevant outcomes; subgroup and recurrence analyses; stimulant vs non-stimulant comparison.
  • Limitations: No data on non-drug therapy; adherence/dose unobserved; potential residual confounding (severity, lifestyle); outcomes limited to medically/legally ascertained events; ADHD presentation not available.
  • Direction of bias: Likely conservative for benefits (adherence misclassification, usual-care comparator, truncation of weights); residual confounding could bias in either direction but negative control reduces concern.
  • Credibility: Pre-registered; RECORD-PE reporting; results align with RCT symptom efficacy and prior observational studies, with plausible attenuated magnitudes.

Clinical and Policy Implications

  • Supports initiating and sustaining ADHD pharmacotherapy to mitigate serious adverse outcomes, especially in those with prior events.
  • Stimulants appear preferable as first-line agents when not contraindicated.
  • Communicate that benefits on accidental injuries may be modest for first events but present for recurrences.
  • Need for pragmatic and representative trials addressing functional and safety outcomes over longer horizons.
